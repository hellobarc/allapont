4. Integrales - Ejercicio 31.1.b

Calcular: \( \displaystyle \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} e^{i2t} dt\)

Existen dos formas de calcular esta integral.

División de números complejos en forma binómica

La división de un número complejo se realiza multiplicando el conjugado del denominador por el numerador y el denominador.

\( \displaystyle \frac{z_1}{z_2} = \frac{z_1 \bar{z_2} }{z_2 \bar{z_2} } \)

Fórmula de Euler

\( e^{i \theta} = \cos \theta + i \sin \theta \)

Prueba 1:

Integramos por sustitución:

\(u=2it\)

\(du = 2idt\)

\(\displaystyle dt = \frac{du}{2i } \)

Reemplazamos en la integral original

\( \begin{aligned} \displaystyle \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} e^{i2t} dt &= \frac{1}{2i} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} e^{u} du \\ &= -\frac{i}{2} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} e^{u} du \\ &= -\frac{i}{2} e^{u} \biggm\vert_0^\frac{\pi}{6}\\ &= -\frac{i}{2} e^{2it} \biggm\vert_0^\frac{\pi}{6}\\ &= -\frac{i}{2} \Big( e^{ \frac{2 i\pi}{6}} - 1 \Big) \\ &= -\frac{i}{2} \Big( e^{ \frac{i\pi}{3}} - 1 \Big) \\ &= -\frac{i}{2} \Big( \cos \Big(\frac{\pi}{3}\Big) + i \sin \Big(\frac{\pi}{3}\Big) - 1 \Big) \\ &= -\frac{i}{2} \Big( \frac{1}{2} + i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} - 1 \Big) \\ &= -\frac{i}{2} \Big( i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} - \frac{1}{2} \Big) \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} + \frac{i}{4} \\ \end{aligned} \)

\(\therefore \displaystyle \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} e^{i2t} dt = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} + \frac{i}{4} \\\)

Prueba 2:

\( \begin{aligned} \displaystyle \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} e^{i2t} dt &= \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \cos (2t) + i \sin (2t) dt \\ &= \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \cos (2t) dt + i \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \sin (2t) dt \\ \end{aligned} \)

Se procede por sustitución

\(u=2t\)

\(du=2dt\)

\(dt=\frac{1}{2}du\)

Se reemplaza en la integral original

\( \begin{aligned} \displaystyle \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \cos (2t) dt + i \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \sin (2t) dt &= \frac{1}{2} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \cos (u) du + \frac{i}{2} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \sin (u) du \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \sin (u) \biggm\vert_0^\frac{\pi}{6} - \frac{i}{2} \cos (u) \biggm\vert_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \sin (2t) \biggm\vert_0^\frac{\pi}{6} - \frac{i}{2} \cos (2t) \biggm\vert_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \Big[ \sin \Big(\frac{\pi}{3} \Big) - \sin (0) \Big] - \frac{i}{2} \Big[ \cos \Big(\frac{\pi}{3} \Big) - \cos(0) \Big] \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} - \frac{i}{2}\Big(\frac{1}{2}-1\Big) \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} - \frac{i}{2}\Big(-\frac{1}{2}\Big) \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} + \frac{i}{4} \\ \end{aligned} \)

\(\therefore \displaystyle \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} e^{i2t} dt = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} + \frac{i}{4} \\\)
